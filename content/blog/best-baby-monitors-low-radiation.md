## What Actually Matters in a Baby Monitor

Before we get into specific products, here's what I wish someone had told me when I was building my registry:

**Radiation and signal type matter more than features.** Wifi monitors are convenient but emit more signal than dedicated radio monitors. If this concerns you — and it concerned me — look for monitors that use DECT or FHSS radio signals, or wifi monitors specifically designed to be mounted far from the crib.

**Camera angle matters more than you'd think.** Top-down views show you the entire crib. Side-mounted cameras miss half the picture. I didn't realize this until I had a camera and couldn't see where my baby had rolled.

**Night vision quality varies wildly.** Read reviews specifically about night vision — that's where budget monitors fall apart.

**Watch for subscription fees.** Some monitors lock their best features behind a monthly fee. Know exactly what you're paying for before you buy.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What baby monitor has the least radiation?
No-wifi monitors like the Infant Optics DXR-8 Pro and Eufy SpaceView Pro use dedicated DECT or FHSS radio signals, which emit significantly less radiation than wifi-based monitors. The Nanit Pro uses wifi but mounts at least 3 feet from the baby, which reduces signal exposure substantially at normal use distances.

### Is the Nanit Pro safe for babies?
Yes. The Nanit is specifically designed to be mounted away from the crib, which keeps any wifi signal exposure minimal. It's used and recommended by many pediatricians. As with all electronics near babies, follow the manufacturer's placement guidelines.

### Do I need breathing tracking on a baby monitor?
Not necessarily. Breathing tracking features like Nanit's aren't medical devices and don't replace safe sleep practices — firm mattress, no loose bedding, baby on their back. But if having it helps you actually sleep, it's worth it. Just know the Nanit Breathing Wear is a separate purchase.

### What is the difference between a wifi and non-wifi baby monitor?
Wifi monitors let you view the feed on your phone from anywhere in the house (or outside it). Non-wifi monitors use a dedicated radio signal between the camera and a handheld parent unit — you can only see the feed on that unit, but radiation is lower and there's no app, no cloud storage, and no subscription fees.

### Is Nanit worth the price?
If you want sleep tracking, a top-down camera view, and breathing monitoring — yes, it's worth it. Budget for the Breathing Wear accessory on top of the camera price. If you want a reliable monitor without the extras, the Infant Optics DXR-8 Pro delivers everything you need at a lower price.
