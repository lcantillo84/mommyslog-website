Let me save you the download-and-delete cycle I went through when my first baby was born.

I tried four different baby tracker apps in the first two weeks. Two of them were "free" until I was three days in and suddenly couldn't see my own history without upgrading. One had ads that kept popping up while I was trying to log a 3am feed with one hand. The last one required creating an account just to open it.

I eventually found what I was looking for, and now I use it with my second baby too. Below is my honest breakdown — the apps that are actually free, what the real limitations are, and what to avoid.

## What "Actually Free" Means

Before we get into the list, let me define terms. A lot of apps call themselves free when they're really:

- **Free to download, pay to use** — you can open it but basic features require a subscription
- **Free tier with ads** — technically free, but you're watching ads at 3am
- **Free trial** — 7 or 14 days, then they charge you
- **Free with account** — requires email signup, which means your data goes to their servers

For this list, I'm focusing on apps where **the features you actually use every day are free with no time limit, no ads, and no account required**.

## The Apps Worth Your Time

### Mommy's Log — My Personal Pick

[Download Mommy's Log free on the App Store →](https://apps.apple.com/us/app/mommys-log/id6755473620)

This is the one I use. I've used it with two kids and I'd download it again in a heartbeat.

**What's free forever:**
- Breastfeeding tracking (left/right, duration, which side is next)
- Bottle and formula logging
- Diaper tracking (wet, dirty, or both)
- Full history by day
- Reminders for feeds and diaper changes
- Works completely offline — no internet needed
- No account, no email, no setup

**What costs money ($2.99/month Pro):**
- Partner sync so both parents see logs in real time
- Advanced patterns and insights view

That's it. Everything you actually need to survive the newborn stage is free. The paid features are genuinely useful if you want them — partner sync is one of the most requested features for two-parent households — but you'll never hit a wall that stops you from logging a feed.

No ads. No account. All your data stays on your phone.

**The honest downside:** iOS only. If you're on Android, this one isn't for you yet.

### Baby Tracker by Amila — Best Free Android Option

*Researched, not personally tested.*

Baby Tracker by Amila is one of the most downloaded baby tracker apps on both iOS and Android. The free tier is genuinely generous — it covers feeding, diaper, sleep, growth, and milestone tracking without requiring a subscription.

**What stands out:**
- Works on both iOS and Android
- Free tier covers all the basics
- Clean simple interface
- Multiple baby profiles

**The tradeoff:** The free version shows ads. Not aggressively, but they're there. Paid version removes them at around $2.99/month.

### Huckleberry — Best for Sleep Tracking

*Researched, not personally tested.*

Huckleberry is best known for its SweetSpot sleep prediction — it analyzes your baby's patterns and tells you the optimal nap time. It's genuinely useful. But it's not really a "free" app.

**What's actually free:**
- Basic feeding and diaper logging
- Limited history (recent entries only)

**What requires payment ($9.99/month):**
- SweetSpot sleep prediction
- Full history access
- Advanced insights

If sleep tracking and predictions are your priority, Huckleberry is excellent — but budget for the subscription. For free core logging, it's the wrong app.

---

### Glow Baby — Best Free with Most Features

*Researched, not personally tested.*

Glow Baby has one of the most feature-rich free tiers in the baby tracker category. Feeding, diaper, sleep, growth, milestones, health records — most of it is free.

**What stands out:**
- Very feature-rich free tier
- Clean design
- Community features (forums, questions)

**The tradeoffs:** Shows ads in the free version. Requires account creation. And the community/social features mean your data is on their servers, not just your phone. If privacy matters to you, this one requires more thought.

### Sprout Baby — Solid Paid Option Worth Mentioning

*Researched, not personally tested.*

Sprout Baby isn't free — it's a one-time purchase around $4.99. I'm including it because if you want a no-subscription app and don't mind paying once, it's highly rated with no ongoing fees.

**What stands out:**
- One-time purchase, no subscription ever
- Works offline
- No ads

If the idea of a monthly fee bothers you more than a one-time cost, Sprout is the alternative.
